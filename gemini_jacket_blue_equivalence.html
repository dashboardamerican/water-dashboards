Sources and references
  • Inspiration - Andy Masley; helpful overview and starting point for framing and identifying sources: Andy Masley writing hub, The AI Water Issue Is Fake, What a Data Center Is.
  • Inspiration - Brian Potter; helpful overview and starting point for framing and identifying sources: Construction Physics, How Does the US Use Water?, I Was Wrong About Data Center Water Use.
  • Google direct prompt water + prompt energy: Google Gemini environmental study (arXiv:2508.15734).
  • LBNL indirect intensity boundary (includes hydro evaporation treatment in that boundary): LBNL US Data Center Energy Usage and Water Consumption.
  • Non-hydro sensitivity path (Potter cross-check framing of ~275 Mgal/day total with hydro excluded): Brian Potter review.
  • Cotton footprints and blue share (10,000 L/kg; 33% blue; 2495 L/shirt): WFN cotton detail.
  • Bovine leather water footprint (17,093 L/kg total; 4% blue share global baseline): Mekonnen & Hoekstra (2012), Water Footprint of Farm Animal Products.
  • US cattle blue water correction (2,275 L/kg blue for US beef, 4.33× M&H global; applied to leather): Klopatek & Oltjen (2022), Journal of Animal Science.
  • California almond blue water footprint (5,290 L/kg blue; 10,240 L/kg total): Fulton et al. (2019), Water-indexed benefits and impacts of California almonds, Ecological Indicators.
  • Lawn sprinkler flow rate (12 GPM typical residential oscillating sprinkler; 720 gal/hr): EPA WaterSense outdoor water use.
  • Sofa upholstery fabric quantity (18 yards of 54-in fabric at 375 GSM ≈ 7.5 kg cotton): Revolution Fabrics yardage guide.
  • Average American annual blue-water baseline used for percentage comparison: USGS modeled CONUS consumptive use = 84,939 Mgal/day (water year 2020), divided by 2020 US population (331,449,281), then annualized: USGS water-use model estimates and US Census 2020 results.
